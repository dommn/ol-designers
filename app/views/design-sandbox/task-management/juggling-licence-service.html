What do these terms mean?

Service URL

if live, provide a link to your service start page on GOV.UK and/or any other relevant pages. Otherwise, provide a link to a prototype or user journey map - including any passwords.

Signed in state URL

The URL for the page you want users to see when they follow the service-card link to your service. For example, https://claim-criminal-injuries-compensation.service.justice.gov.uk/account

This type of page might be:

  • a dashboard
  • a service page where the they can pick up from where they were before
  • a page they see after they’ve created an account or signed in

GOV.UK start page URL

The URL of your service’s starting point on GOV.UK. For example, https://www.gov.uk/claim-compensation-criminal-injury/make-claim

Anyone should be able to access this page, not just signed-in users.

In most cases, this page will be a link on GOV.UK rather than a page on your service domain. It’s used in lists of services currently using GOV.UK One Login.
