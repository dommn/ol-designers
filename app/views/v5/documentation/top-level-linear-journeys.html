Sign in journeys

Create a GOV.UK One Login or let your user sign in with an existing one at the start of your service journey

This journey shows your user creating a GOV.UK One Login at the start of your service journey.

Your user begins their journey on your service’s start page, usually on GOV.UK. They create a GOV.UK One Login using their email address, a password and two-factor authentication. Then, they’re sent to your service to complete their journey.

This option is usually best for your service, if you need all your users to create a GOV.UK One Login before they can do anything.

Create a GOV.UK One Login or let your user sign in with an existing one to save their progress

This journey shows a user creating a GOV.UK One Login in the middle of your service journey. This allows them to save and continue later.

Users start at your service’s start page, usually on GOV.UK. They complete part of your journey, then decide they want to finish it later.

To save their progress, they create a GOV.UK One Login using their email address, a password and two-factor authentication.

Later, they return to your service and resume their journey by signing in to their GOV.UK One Login.

This option is usually best for your service if you do not need all your users to create a GOV.UK One Login, but want to give them the option of saving their progress.

Sign in and identity checking journeys

Check the identity of your user who has a passport with an NFC chip and is using the GOV.UK ID Check app

This journey shows your user completing the whole journey in their web browser and also using the GOV.UK ID Check app. They have a passport with an NFC chip in it and are sent to prove their identity using the GOV.UK ID Check app.

Your user begins their journey in your service, where they’re asked to prove their identity.

They sign in or create a GOV.UK One Login, then answer some questions about their device and type of document.

Then they’re sent to the GOV.UK ID check app. They do a biometric identity document check then a biometric face check.

Afterwards, they return to GOV.UK One Login to complete an address and fraud check. They have now completed the process of proving their identity.

Then, they’re sent back to your service and continue the rest of their journey.

Check the identity of your user who has a passport without an NFC chip and is answering security questions

This journey shows your user completing the whole journey in their web browser. They have a passport without an NFC chip and are prompted to answer security questions.

Your user begins their journey in your service, where they’re asked to prove their identity.

They sign in or create a GOV.UK One Login, then answer some questions about their device and type of document.

They’re asked to enter their passport details and then complete an address and fraud check. Then they answer some security questions which only they will know the answer to.

They have now completed the process of proving their identity.

Then, they’re sent back to your service and continue the rest of their journey.

The signed in experience

Sign in to see your GOV.UK One Login

Your user’s GOV.UK One Login includes an area where they can change their sign in details. They can also see which services they’ve accessed with their GOV.UK One Login.

Your user begins their journey in your service and are then sent to their GOV.UK One Login.

They sign into their GOV.UK One Login. In one central area, they can see and access links to all the services that they’ve previously used.

They can choose the service link to take them to the service they want to use.

Once they have completed their journey or transaction, they can sign out of your service, or can choose to return back to their GOV.UK One Login.

Sign in to change your email address and security details

Your user can navigate from your service to their GOV.UK One Login to change their sign in details.

Your user begins their journey in your service and are then sent to their GOV.UK One Login.

They sign into their GOV.UK One Login. They choose the Change credentials link. This takes them to a screen where they can update their email address and security details.

Once they’ve done this, they can sign out of their GOV.UK One Login.
