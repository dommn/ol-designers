    • Checking users’ identities

    As well as letting users sign in, you can use GOV.UK One Login to carry out identity checks on your users.

    Users will prove their identity once. They can reuse this proof to access other services that use GOV.UK One Login - saving them time and reducing duplication of effort across government.

    We’ve designed the service following the government guidance on how to prove and verify someone’s identity (GPG 45).

    Identity is currently not available for services that are not already in our identity private beta. Join our mailing list to be updated when it’s available for more services.

    The user experience

    There are multiple ways we can check a user’s identity. There are multiple ways we can check a user’s identity. This is because users will have different documentation, evidence and preferences.

    Evidence users need to provide

    All users need to provide:

    • their name and date of birth
    • their address history for the last 3 years
    • a photo ID

    The types of photo ID we currently accept are:

    • a passport from the UK
    • a passport with an NFC chip from another country
    • a driving licence from the UK or Northern Ireland

    Some users will need to answer knowledge based verification questions (KBVs). These are security questions generated based on their credit record.

    In future, we’ll expand the types of evidence we accept so people can prove their identity without needing a photo ID. This is to make the service more inclusive.

    Channels available

    Users can use their web browser or the GOV.UK ID Check app.

    We’re looking into other channels, for example, offline checks.

    We currently provide a medium level of confidence

    Our current journeys will give you a medium level of confidence, as defined by GPG45. This means it will lower the risk of you accepting:

    • completely made-up (’synthetic’) identities
    • impostors who do not have a relationship with the claimed identity - for example, someone who has found the claimed identity’s information on social media
    • impostors who have information about the claimed identity that’s not in the public domain - for example, someone who works for the claimed identity’s employer’s HR department using information they have to impersonate the claimed identity

    In future we will offer low and high confidence too.

    Deciding if this level is right for your service

    To decide if this level of confidence is right for your service, you need to identify the risks to your service and see if the medium level of confidence mitigates them.

    If you’re not sure, we’ll be happy to discuss it with you. Get in touch through our support form or Slack channel.

    Identity data your service can get about your users

    User attributes

    If a user successfully proves their identity, you will always receive some core identity information about them:

    • their names
    • dates of birth
    • the level of identity confidence

    You can also ask for:

    • postal addresses for the last 3 years (we check that addresses are genuine and if they’re associated with fraudulent activity)
    • passport details, if the user proved their identity using their passport
    • driving licence details, if the user proved their identity using their driving licence

    We may ask you to provide a reason for requesting these pieces of information, so we can avoid unnecessary data sharing.

    This is in addition to the data you’ll get if you also use the ‘sign in’ part of GOV.UK One Login, which is:

    • a unique identifier
    • an email address
    • a phone number, if the user set up two-factor authentication using their phone number

    If there’s any other data your service needs - get in touch and ask us about it.

    Audit and fraud data

    If an account has shown evidence of being fraudulent, we can provide more information about that user.
